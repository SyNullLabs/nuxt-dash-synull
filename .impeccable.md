## Design Context

### Users

This dashboard is for end users rather than internal operators. They come here to purchase products, manage the products they already own, and complete routine account and service-management tasks with as little friction as possible.

### Brand Personality

The product should feel simple, technological, and easy to use. The interface should create a sense of confidence and clarity, helping users feel in control of purchases and product management without visual noise or cognitive overload.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use `../synull-www` as the primary visual reference, but adapt it for a product dashboard instead of a marketing site. The experience should default to dark mode and borrow the reference project's stronger visual DNA: dark surfaces, polished depth, and a modern technical tone. Keep the execution restrained and functional so it feels product-grade rather than decorative.

### Design Principles

1. Default to a dark-first interface with strong contrast and clear hierarchy.
2. Borrow the visual language of `synull-www`, but simplify it for task-focused product management flows.
3. Prioritize clarity and speed over decoration: key actions, statuses, and product data should always be obvious.
4. Keep the interface feeling modern and technical, but never intimidating or overly dense.
5. Make common user actions such as browsing products, purchasing, and managing services feel straightforward on both desktop and mobile.
